# MudClub Architecture Handbook
## 09. Roadmap

Version: 1.0
Status: Living Document

---

# Purpose

This document outlines the long-term direction of MudClub.

Unlike the remainder of the Architecture Handbook, this document is expected to evolve regularly as the project matures.

The roadmap describes aspirations rather than commitments.

Implementation priorities may change according to community feedback and practical experience.

---

# Vision

Develop MudClub into a modular platform capable of supporting a wide range of amateur sports organisations while preserving a coherent and understandable architecture.

---

# Core

Future improvements include:

- temporal memberships;
- richer organisational roles;
- improved permissions;
- multi-club participation;
- configurable organisational structures.

---

# Registrations

Future improvements include:

- public registration forms;
- admissions workflow;
- document management;
- parental approvals;
- waiting lists;
- automated communications.

---

# Coaching

Future improvements include:

- player development plans;
- practice planning;
- coaching journals;
- attendance analytics;
- skill progression.

---

# Competition

Future improvements include:

- multiple competition formats;
- tournament management;
- standings;
- statistics;
- federation integration.

---

# Finance

Potential capabilities:

- subscriptions;
- invoicing;
- online payments;
- budgeting;
- sponsorship;
- grants.

---

# Communications

Potential capabilities:

- announcements;
- messaging;
- newsletters;
- push notifications;
- event reminders.

---

# Facilities

Potential capabilities:

- facility management;
- bookings;
- equipment allocation;
- maintenance scheduling.

---

# Platform

Potential capabilities:

- REST API;
- GraphQL;
- plugin architecture;
- mobile applications;
- import/export;
- authentication providers.

---

# Sports

Continue evolving towards configuration-driven sports support rather than sport-specific implementations.

The objective is to allow new sports to be introduced primarily through configuration.

---

# Community

Long-term objectives include:

- contributor documentation;
- extension guides;
- translations;
- sample clubs;
- demonstration data.

---

# Success

MudClub will be considered successful when:

- clubs enjoy using it;
- contributors enjoy extending it;
- new sports can be supported with minimal development;
- the architecture remains understandable after many years of evolution.
